“Why Not Just Use ChatGPT?”

Everybody asks. Here’s the honest answer.

The Hallucination Problem

It fabricates numbers with perfect confidence.

An LLM will invent a revenue figure or a covenant threshold and present it with the same certainty as a real extraction. In a deal context, one wrong number shifts millions—and you won’t know which ones to trust without checking every single output. That defeats the purpose.

The Speed Wall

It breaks at deal speed.

Try running 500 data room PDFs through ChatGPT. By document 30 you hit: “Your usage limit has been reached. Try again Wednesday.” Meanwhile your deal closes Friday. Our Python pipelines process the same 500 documents in hours—no rate limits, no waiting, no per-query charges eating your budget.

The Expertise Gap

A power tool is not a system.

Handing an LLM to an analyst is like handing a circular saw to someone who’s never built a house. Impressive and dangerous. Out of 900 million weekly AI users, very few can extract reliable value. We’ve been teaching LLM-based financial analysis to Masters students and professionals—the failure modes are systematic and repeatable.

What we build instead

Engineered Python pipelines that use AI strategically—for intelligent scanning, classification, and pattern recognition—inside workflows that compute every number directly from source documents, maintain full audit trails, and flag the 1–5% of ambiguous cases for mandatory human review. The result is more accurate than AI alone, humans alone, or AI plus inexperienced humans.
